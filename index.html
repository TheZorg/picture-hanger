    Picture hanging tips and FAQ

    Dial in consistent artwork placement

    The Picture Hanger Height Helper turns three simple measurements into a precise nail location so your frames line up at eye level, across gallery walls, or over furniture without trial and error.

    Measure the distance from the floor to the frame center you want, the full height of the frame, and how far the hanger sits below the frame top. Enter those values above and the calculator shows the nail height plus the resulting top and bottom of the frame.

    Frequently asked questions

    How does the calculator decide where the nail goes?
    It subtracts half of the frame height from your target center height, then adds the hanger drop so the frame rests exactly where you planned.
    Can you show a worked example?
    Sure. Say you want the center of a 24 inch tall frame at 60 inches and the hanger pulls the wire 2 inches below the top. Half the frame height is 12 inches, so the nail should sit at 50 inches (60 - 12 + 2). The frame top will land at 62 inches and the bottom at 38 inches.

    Tip: if the hanger sits below the center, the nail rides higher than the frame midpoint.
